# Voice and Trust Guidelines

## Audience

People who are skeptical, streetwise, and have zero patience for corporate spin.

## Tone Rules

- Be direct and plainspoken.
- Explain why we ask for information before we ask.
- Say what happens with data in concrete terms.
- Keep agency with the user: "your choice", "you can skip".

## Avoid

- Therapy buzzwords without explanation.
- Corporate language ("optimize", "engagement", "frictionless").
- Empty reassurance ("we care deeply").
- Hidden implications about privacy or outcomes.

## UI Copy Patterns

- **Onboarding**: "Start a one-time meeting now. No account required."
- **Input context**: "Why we ask: this helps the room talk to you like a person."
- **Errors**: "Service is down on our side. Try again in a minute."
- **Account value**: "Create an account only if you want saved history."

## Trust Checklist for Reviews

- Does every requested input have a plain-language "why"?
- Can users start a one-off flow without account creation?
- Do error messages avoid leaking key names or stack traces?
- Is copy free of manipulative or overly polished language?
